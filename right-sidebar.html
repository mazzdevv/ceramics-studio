New classes and events posted weekly.

  • Flirty Feet

    Learn how to trim delicate, balanced, and clean feet onto all your ceramic creations. All bowls/cups/vases deserve a beautiful foot! Led by Amanda Wright.

    Trimming feet, like all aspects of ceramics, is molded to your own personal style. Some people enjoy a tall foot and others prefer none at all. This class explores different types of feet and how to approach them!

  • In a Pinch

    Not into throwing? Need a break from the wheel? Maybe you need a pinch pot class! Vlad is leading a class on making usable ceramics with just your fingers and some clay!

    Pinch pots are a classic introduction into the art of using clay to make three dimensional artifacts. We find that it is an excellent way to reconnect with the clay and find joy in imperfection.

  • Wednesday Wedging

    Learn the art of removing air pockets and reclaiming scrap clay with wedging! If youve kneaded bread before maybe this will seem familiar... Led by Lucas Harman on (you guessed it) Wednesdays.

    Be ready for a workout! This weekly class is not only informative, but economical as well. If you are a regular and don't want to keep loosing clay to your trimmings and slip, reclaiming your clay is going to let you make the clay you have last longer!

  • Tiles of Lisboa

    Inspired by a trip to Lisbon, Alice Liu is teaching you how to paint inticate tiles so you can bring a taste of Portugal to your own home!

    As a special addition to this class, Alice is bringing some treats and snacks inspired by her favorite Portuguese eats! Pasteis de Nata, croquettes, and tinned fish with fresh bread.

  • Plant Pressing

    Do you have a plant that strikes your fancy? Why no imortalize its patterns in a slab of clay? This pressed clay can be used for slab mugs, dishes, coasters, and more! Led by Anisa Akanna.

    You can bring your own plants to this class or we will have some interesting specimens for sharing as well.

  • Looks Good On Paper

    Interested in expanding your artistic ventures off the 3D and into the 2D? Jaques Tobar is leading a sight drawing class to stretch your creativity and see how it can affect your ceramic ways of thinking.

    The class will be 2 hours long but participants are welcome to spend all day drawing with your fellow artists.

  • Plants and Pottery

    If you are a plant parent, this course is for you. This is a special three part series for throwing your own planters, intricate glazing, and planting your very own plant into it! Ceramics and plants go hand in hand so why not display your art and plants together! Led by plant and pottery enthusiast, Marco Denery.

    As a more popular course, each of the individual classes will have two different sessions, an early afternoon and an evening option. The first three Sundays of the month are when the classes will be held.

  • Wine Tasting

    Hey, you! Trying to connect with the community but find it hard to chat while in the zone of throwing/trimming/glazing/etc? Well this is for you! Join us at a little wine tasting meant to bring the community together over some local sips.

    Wine kindly provided by a local wine shop. Non-alcoholic wines will also be available.

  • Artisan Market

    Our monthly artisan market takes place on the first Saturday of every month! Browse the wares of local artists and/or set up a little stand to sell your own!

    Market takes place on the main drag by our studio from 11am to 3pm
