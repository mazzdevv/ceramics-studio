Who are we?

Hi there! We are a group of artists who want to bring a welcoming and accessible art space to our community. We encourage teaching and learning from each other.

What do we do?

We have put together a ceramics based studio (but feel free to use this space for other artistic endeavors or for the community) for the people!

Why do we do this?

Too much of our society is based on buying and selling manufactured goods. Why not get together with a group of amazing people and create, not only pots, but memories.

Would you like to make something... With us?

More About Logistics

Memberships?

In order to make our studio accessible, we have a sliding scale for membership prices. You can choose what you want to pay based on how much you think you will use the space and how much you can afford.

Courses/Workshops?

Courses and Workshops are free for members though we encourage giving back either to the instructor (with snacks or gifts depending on the person) or to the community.

  • Tell Me More

Some of our upcoming classes/events!

  • Flirty Feet

    Learn how to trim delicate, balanced, and clean feet onto all your ceramic creations. All bowls/cups/vases deserve a beautiful foot! Led by Amanda Wright.

  • In a Pinch

    Not into throwing? Need a break from the wheel? Maybe you need a pinch pot class! Vlad is leading a class on making usable ceramics with just your fingers and some clay!

  • Wednesday Wedging

    Learn the art of removing air pockets and reclaiming scrap clay with wedging! If youve kneaded bread before maybe this will seem familiar... Led by Lucas Harman on (you guessed it) Wednesdays.

  • Tiles of Lisboa

    Inspired by a trip to Lisbon, Alice Liu is teaching you how to paint inticate tiles so you can bring a taste of Portugal to your own home!

  • Plant Pressing

    Do you have a plant that strikes your fancy? Why no imortalize its patterns in a slab of clay? This pressed clay can be used for slab mugs, dishes, coasters, and more! Led by Anisa Akanna.

  • Looks Good On Paper

    Interested in expanding your artistic ventures off the 3D and into the 2D? Jaques Tobar is leading a sight drawing class to stretch your creativity and see how it can affect your ceramic ways of thinking.
